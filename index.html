Project Overview

Challenge

As a group, we were assigned to redesign the user interface homepage for the government agency website, Agriculture & Agri-Food Canada, while following Responsive Web Design standards. Along with redesigning the homepage, we were advised to select four unique pages in the current website to remodel.

Summary

Agriculture & Agri-Food Canada is a collaborative platform designed to provide farmers, food processors and distributors access to information on youth employment programs, asset protection in the form of insurance and updated information surrounding international trade barriers and funding.

Based from the current design of the Agriculture & Agri-Food Canada website, we selected four important pages under mostly requested section to redesign, which were the Youth Employment screen, COVID-19 Information sector, International Trade screen, and Indigenous People screen.

Design Process

Agriculture & Agri-Food Canada is a collaborative platform designed to provide farmers, food processors and distributors access to information on youth employment programs, asset protection in the form of insurance and updated information surrounding international trade barriers and funding.

Based from the current design of the Agriculture & Agri-Food Canada website, we selected four important pages under mostly requested section to redesign, which were the Youth Employment screen, COVID-19 Information sector, International Trade screen, and Indigenous People screen.

The Problem

Poor navigation and limited UI features on the Agriculture & Agri-Food Canada website leading to a decrease in consumer retention and user traffic to the site's resources.

The Solution

Iterated and produced clear primary, secondary and footer navigation, while, heightening visual appeal, and bolstering brand recognition.

Design Inspiration

Branding

We took an approach to develop the kind of agricultural/farming environment to present to our users, in order to feel welcomed and well at home. We sought to find visuals and colours to best keep the mood of the website displayed as a calming state for our users. We add various splashes of colour such as green to symbolize vegetables and crops. We decided to center it black with white for the font text colour to best capture appeal and legibility. User Test Feedback

After planning our layout, we developed a Mid-fi version of an improved functional prototype of Agriculture & Agri-Food Canada. We conducted usability tests after making a few iterations to it to finally evolve it into our High-fi Prototype (both a webpage and a mobile prototype).

We took in the following input and observations during our second stage of testing for our Mid-fi prototype.

    - Smoother navigation throughout site.
    - Decrease of unnecessary links.
    - Categorization of information and displaying a limited amount of options at once assisted the user.
    - Aesthetically pleasing.
    - Hover feature allowed for the user to be oriented to a specific link or secondary page.
    - Simple layout bolstered a pleasurable experience for the user.
